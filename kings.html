Great Kings of Myanmar

The visionary monarchs who shaped a nation's destiny

King Anawrahta

Reign: 1044–1077

Founder of the Pagan Empire who unified Myanmar and established Theravada Buddhism.

  • Founded first Burmese empire
  • Built thousands of temples
  • Established Burmese script
King Kyansittha

Reign: 1084–1112

Continued Anawrahta's work, completing religious buildings and strengthening the empire.

  • Completed Ananda Temple
  • Expanded trade relations
  • Patron of literature
King Bayinnaung

Reign: 1551–1581

Created the largest empire in Southeast Asian history through military conquests.

  • Conquered much of SE Asia
  • Established legal code
  • Religious tolerance
King Alaungpaya

Reign: 1752–1760

Founder of the Konbaung Dynasty who reunified Burma and resisted foreign invasions.

  • Last Burmese dynasty
  • Repelled Chinese invasions
  • Expanded territory
King Hsinbyushin

Reign: 1763–1776

Known for military campaigns against Siam and China, plus cultural achievements.

  • Sacked Ayutthaya
  • Repelled Chinese invasions
  • Arts patron
King Mindon

Reign: 1853–1878

Modernizing monarch who adapted to British colonial pressures while preserving independence.

  • Founded Mandalay
  • Fifth Buddhist Council
  • Modernized administration
