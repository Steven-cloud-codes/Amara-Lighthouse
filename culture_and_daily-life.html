What is Thanakha?

Thanakha is a traditional cosmetic paste made from the ground bark of certain trees, mainly the Thanakha tree (Murraya or Limonia acidissima), commonly found in Myanmar. It has been used by Myanmar people for over 2,000 years as both a natural skincare product and a cultural symbol.

Traditional Clothing of Myanmar

The elegant attire reflecting Myanmar's rich cultural heritage

Men's Attire

Longyi Taikpon Gaung Baung

Myanmar men traditionally wear the longyi (sarong) paired with a taikpon (shirt) and gaung baung (headwrap) for formal occasions. Daily wear is simpler with cotton longyis.

Women's Attire

Htaingmathein Longyi Eingyi

Women wear elegant htaingmathein (blouses) with longyi (sarongs), often accessorized with shawls or eingyi (jackets). Fabrics feature intricate patterns and vibrant colors.

Traditional Musical Instruments

Saung Gauk

The national instrument of Myanmar, a 13-string harp with distinctive curved neck, traditionally used in royal courts and classical music performances.

Pattala

A bamboo xylophone with 24 slats, played with two padded mallets. It produces a bright, resonant sound and is essential in traditional ensembles.

Hne

A double-reed oboe-like instrument made of bamboo with a brass bell. Its piercing sound leads traditional orchestras and accompanies dramatic performances.

Sai Waa (Sine Waing)

A circular frame of 21 tuned drums, played with great skill to produce melodic patterns. The centerpiece of traditional orchestras, creating rich rhythmic textures.

Traditional Dance & Performance Arts

Myanmar's rich performance traditions include classical dances, folk dances, and puppetry.

Classical Dance

Myanmar's classical dance features graceful, controlled movements with intricate hand gestures and elaborate costumes, often depicting Jataka tales and royal court scenes.

Folk & Festival Dances

Includes regional dances like the Chin bamboo dance, Kachin manau circle dance, and the Elephant dance of Kyaukse performed during local festivals.
