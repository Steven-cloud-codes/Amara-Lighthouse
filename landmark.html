Myanmar Landmarks

Discover the cultural treasures of Myanmar

Landmarks are crucial for orientation, providing cognitive anchors and reference points that aid in wayfinding and communication. They also contribute to a sense of place and cultural identity, shaping how we understand and interact with our environment.

Bagan

  • Ancient city with over 2,000 Buddhist temples and pagodas
  • Flourished between 11th-13th centuries as capital of Pagan Kingdom
  • Features famous temples like Ananda, Dhammayangyi, and Thatbyinnyu
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2019
Watch Video

Mandalay Palace

  • Last royal palace of the last Burmese monarchy
  • Built between 1857-1859 by King Mindon
  • Surrounded by 4km of walls and a moat
  • Features the impressive Watchtower and Glass Palace
Watch Video

Kyaiktiyo (Golden Rock)

  • Famous Buddhist pilgrimage site with a golden boulder balancing on a cliff
  • Believed to be held in place by a strand of Buddha's hair
  • Located near Kyaikto in Mon State, about 160km from Yangon
  • Pilgrims cover the rock with gold leaf as an act of devotion
Watch Video

Inle Lake

  • Second largest lake in Myanmar at 22 km long
  • Famous for Intha leg-rowing fishermen technique
  • Home to floating villages and gardens
  • Phaung Daw Oo Pagoda festival happens annually
Watch Video

U Bein Bridge

  • World's longest teakwood bridge at 1.2 km
  • Built in 1850s using reclaimed teak from former royal palace
  • Crosses Taungthaman Lake near Amarapura
  • Most photographed at sunset with silhouetted monks
Watch Video

Hpa An

  • Capital of Kayin State with stunning karst landscapes
  • Sacred Mount Zwegabin dominates the skyline
  • Kawgun Cave features thousands of Buddha images
  • Lumbini Garden has 1,000 identical Buddha statues
Watch Video

Mount Popa

  • Volcanic peak rising 1,518 meters above sea level
  • Home to 37 Mahagiri Nats (animist spirits)
  • 777 steps lead to Popa Taungkalat monastery
  • Considered the "Mount Olympus of Myanmar"
Watch Video
