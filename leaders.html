General Aung San (1915-1947)

Father of modern Myanmar and independence leader

  • • Born February 13, 1915 in Natmauk, Burma
  • • Founded the Communist Party of Burma in 1939
  • • Established the Burma Independence Army during WWII
  • • Negotiated the Panglong Agreement (1947) unifying ethnic groups
  • • Assassinated July 19, 1947 along with six cabinet members
  • • Father of Nobel laureate Aung San Suu Kyi
Key Achievements:

Led Burma's independence movement from British rule, formed the basis for the modern Burmese military (Tatmadaw), and created a framework for national unity through the Panglong Agreement.

Other Leaders

U Thant

(1909–1974) was a Burmese diplomat and the third Secretary-General of the United Nations, serving from 1961 to 1971. He was the first Asian to hold this position. Born in Pantanaw, British Burma, U Thant was a schoolteacher and journalist before entering government service.

U Nu

(1907–1995) was the first Prime Minister of independent Burma, serving from 1948 to 1956 and again in the 1960s. A close ally of General Aung San, he promoted democracy and Buddhism in post-independence Myanmar.
